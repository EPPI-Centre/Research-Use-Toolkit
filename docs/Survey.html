Decision-makers’ Research Use Toolkit

Aims of the toolkit [This is WORK in progress!]

  • To consider the components of using research evidence (walk through the different components of research use)
  • To provide Links to further resources on specific aspects of these processes
  • To Record your progress in following your own strategy of using research

Stages of developing and monitoring the use of research

Clarifying:

  1. The topic / issue (where research may help)
  2. The need for research evidence (level of use of research)
  3. The type of research evidence
  4. Accessing relevant research evidence (as defined by ‘2’)
  5. Appraising the research evidence identified
  6. Designing a strategy to support the use of research (barriers & facilitators, mechanisms, behavioural change, & activities)
  7. Monitoring the use of research evidence use (progress in developing and following a strategy
  8. Evaluating the use of research evidence use (what has been the impact?)

2.3 Who and to what extent? In relation to the previously described sequence and level of decision making

Please tick those which may apply:

To be aware of research.

Your awareness that particular bodies of research exist and might be relevant to your decision-making needs.
It does not require you to have access to this research or to have read it and merely refers to your awareness of research.

To engage with research.

Accessing individual research studies or bodies of research and understanding their research results and potential implications for decision-making.
You might have ideas for how these results could inform actual decisions and practices but there is no tangible efforts to integrate research into these.
You might want to be aware of the limitations of the research in terms of method or relevance (including how it has been used in decision making).

To integrate research into decision-making processes.

Tangible efforts to integrate research findings into decision-making processes.
For example: feeding the findings of evaluation studies into policy design; developing professional practice; or setting up systems to foster the continued use of evidence.
